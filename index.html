About

ANova Hacks 2018 is a 24-hour hackathon for high school students run by ANova. It will be held starting Friday, March 16th at 6:00pm at Covo in San Francisco. Over the course of the day, students will work in teams of four to design and code a challenging project of their choosing.

The Hacker Experience

Teams can opt to have an ANova mentor who will guide them throughout the day, from generating project ideas and collaborating to implementing those ideas and polishing a final product that will be showcased during the judging process. Prizes will be awarded to the top teams.

This is a great opportunity for our students to not only apply what they have been learning in the classroom, but also be exposed to crucial skills used in industry and have a better understanding of life after high school.

Our Students

At the Spring 2017 ANova Hacks, we welcomed over 120 students from high schools all over the Bay. This diverse group of hackers was composed primarily of students from under-resourced high schools.

ANova Hacks provides the unique opportunity to meet and connect with others while being exposed to cutting-edge technologies used in both industry and academia. This intersectional experience yields new ideas and approaches to traditional problems.

Workshops and Panels

ANova Hacks provides beginner-friendly and intermediate workshops over the course of the hackathon, ranging from general college informational workshops, to demos in the Berkeley Robotics Lab and demos of Virtual Reality hardware, to an intro workshop in mobile application development (Android.)

We also offer transitional workshops from block coding (i.e. Scratch, Snap) to languages used in industry (i.e. Python, Java). ANova Hacks aims to cater to each student's needs regardless of their experience, so that by the end of the day, they will have a finished product to show-off to their friends and family.

FAQ

Hello students and parents! With ANova Hacks coming up, we thought we’d answer a couple questions you might have regarding logistics in terms of the event. Hopefully, this will ease any concerns, and remember that if there are any questions still up in the air, you may always email us at executive@berkeleyanova.org

  • What is ANova Hacks?

    24-hour hackathon hosted by Berkeley ANova for high school students

    Opportunity for kids to see their projects come to life

    Provide a space for students to learn valuable skills in workshops like web design, college admissions, etc.

  • Why should my child go to ANova Hacks?

    To collaborate with other students with similar interests to work on cool projects they won’t have the time/resources to elsewhere.

    To learn valuable skills in computer science from qualified UC Berkeley mentors.

  • Where will ANova Hacks be, and is the location safe?

    Covo, San Francisco. Indoors, very comfortable

    Located very close to Powell and Civic Center BART stations just three blocks from Market

  • What will be provided for my child at ANova Hacks? (ie. food, sleeping, swag, ect)

    Dinner, breakfast, lunch, and snacks will be provided.

    Mentors to give advice and guidance in their projects

    Free swag from sponsors

  • Who will be in charge of my child at ANova Hacks?

    ANova mentors (all highly qualified, medical experience available, etc.)

    Students will be monitored 24/7, and exiting Covo will be closely watched with ID systems, check-out systems

    Hired security

  • How will my child get to/from ANova Hacks?

    Mentors will be waiting at BART stations to pick students and to drop them off

  • What if my child cannot bring a laptop?

    Partnering with Facebook to provide students with laptops to use.

    Laptops contain top-of-the-line software that students will need at the hackathon.

  • Are there plans in case of an emergency? (ie. evacuation, medical, etc.)

    Every ANova staff member will be equipped with medical information on the child

    All staff will have the appropriate phone numbers to call in case of an emergency
